# Student Housing in the Netherlands

## Overview
Finding housing is one of the biggest challenges for international students in the Netherlands. Dutch cities — especially Amsterdam, Leiden, and Rotterdam — face severe housing shortages. It's critical to start searching as early as possible, ideally 6 months before your arrival.

## Types of Student Accommodation

### 1. University-Affiliated Housing
Many universities partner with housing corporations to provide rooms for (international) students:

- **SSH (Stichting Studenten Huisvesting)** — Operates in Leiden, Delft, Utrecht, Groningen. Offers furnished rooms from ~€350–600/month
- **DUWO** — Active in Delft, Leiden, Amsterdam. Average room: €400–700/month
- **De Key** — Amsterdam-based, serves UvA and HvA students
- **Vestia / RoSH** — Rotterdam-based student housing

**How to Apply:**
1. Register on the housing provider's waiting list as early as possible (some have multi-year waits)
2. Your university's International Office usually provides housing office contacts
3. Some universities guarantee first-year international students a room — check your offer letter

### 2. Private Rental Platforms
- **Kamernet.nl** — Largest room rental platform in the Netherlands. Subscription ~€25/month to contact landlords
- **Room.nl** — Free registration; allocates rooms by lottery or registration time. Long waiting lists
- **HousingAnywhere.com** — Specialises in short-term (1–12 months) furnished rooms for internationals
- **Pararius.nl** — For self-contained apartments (more expensive)
- **Funda.nl** — The main Dutch real estate site

### 3. Facebook Groups
- "Rooms for Rent Amsterdam", "Housing Leiden International Students", etc.
- Be extremely careful of scams — never transfer money without seeing the property or using a rental contract

## Average Monthly Costs (2024)

| Type | City | Monthly Rent |
|---|---|---|
| Student room (shared) | Amsterdam | €600–900 |
| Student room (shared) | Leiden | €400–650 |
| Student room (shared) | Rotterdam | €400–650 |
| Studio apartment | Amsterdam | €1,100–1,800 |
| Student room (SSH) | Delft | €350–550 |

Note: Prices have risen sharply due to housing shortages.

## Additional Costs
- **Service costs (servicekosten)**: Often €50–150/month extra (covers heating, water, internet)
- **Security deposit (borg)**: Usually 1–2 months' rent, paid upfront and returned at end of tenancy
- **Furnishing**: Student rooms are often furnished, but not always. Budget €500–1000 for basics

## Rental Contract Essentials
Always get a written rental contract (huurcontract). Key items to check:
- Monthly rent amount and what it includes
- Contract duration (fixed-term or indefinite)
- Deposit amount and conditions for return
- Notice period (usually 1–3 months)
- House rules (no smoking, guests policy, etc.)
- Landlord's responsibilities for maintenance

**Never sign a contract without reading it fully. Use Google Translate if needed.**

## Things to Watch Out For
- **Scams**: If a deal looks too good to be true, it probably is. Never send money via Western Union or bitcoin
- **Unofficial letting**: Subletting without landlord permission is illegal and can result in eviction
- **No contract**: Always insist on a written contract

## Gemeente (Municipality) Registration
Once you have a permanent address, you must register at the local municipality (gemeente). This gives you:
- BSN (citizen service number)
- Official Dutch address
- Access to healthcare, banking, etc.

## Tips for Finding a Room
1. Start searching 3–6 months before arrival
2. Register on multiple platforms simultaneously
3. Prepare a good introduction letter (who you are, your study, your habits)
4. Video calls with prospective landlords build trust
5. Your university's international office often has emergency/short-term housing options
6. Avoid paying agency fees > 1 month's rent (banned by Dutch law since 2021)
