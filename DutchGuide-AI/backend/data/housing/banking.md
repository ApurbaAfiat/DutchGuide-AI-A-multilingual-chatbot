# Banking in the Netherlands

## Overview
Having a Dutch bank account is essential for daily life — paying rent, receiving salary, and shopping online. Most Dutch online payments use **iDEAL**, which requires a Dutch bank account.

## Getting a Bank Account

### Requirements
- BSN (Burgerservicenummer) — mandatory
- Valid passport or ID card
- Dutch address
- Phone number

### How to Open an Account
1. Get your BSN first
2. Visit a bank branch with your BSN, passport, and proof of address
3. Or apply online (ING and ABN AMRO have online applications for residents)
4. Account + debit card arrives within 3–5 working days

## iDEAL
iDEAL is the dominant Dutch online payment method. Used for:
- Online shopping
- Rent payments
- Government fees
- Utility bills

It works by redirecting you to your bank's app/website to authorise payment. You cannot use iDEAL without a Dutch bank account.

## Dutch Bank Account IBAN Format
Dutch IBANs begin with NL: `NL91 ABNA 0417 1643 00`

## ATMs (Geldautomaat)
- Most ATMs are free to use with Dutch cards
- International cards: check your bank's foreign transaction fees
- ATMs are called "geldautomaat" — widely available in supermarkets and city centres

## Debit Cards
- The standard Dutch debit card is called a **betaalpas** or **maestro** card
- Almost all shops, restaurants, and supermarkets accept card payment
- Contactless payment (tap to pay) is universally accepted

## Useful Tips
- Download your bank's app — Dutch banking apps are excellent
- Set up automatic payments (automatische incasso) for recurring bills
- Keep your IBAN handy — employers, landlords, and government all need it
- Some small businesses still prefer cash (especially markets, small cafés)
