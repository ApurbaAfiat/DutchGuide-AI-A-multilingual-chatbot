# Trams, Buses & Metro in the Netherlands

## City-Level Public Transport Operators

Each major Dutch city has its own local transport authority:

| City | Operator | Modes |
|---|---|---|
| Amsterdam | GVB | Tram, Bus, Metro, Ferry |
| Rotterdam | RET | Metro, Tram, Bus, Water bus |
| The Hague | HTM | Tram, Bus |
| Utrecht | U-OV | Bus |
| Eindhoven | Hermes | Bus |

## Amsterdam (GVB)

### Metro
- **Line 50 (Ring)** — Connects Amsterdam west and southeast
- **Line 51, 53, 54** — Run from Central Station to southeast Amsterdam
- **Line 52 (North-South line)** — Runs from Amsterdam Noord through Central Station to Amstel

### Trams
- 14 tram lines covering the city centre and neighbourhoods
- Line 2, 11, 12 — Central Station to Museumplein (Van Gogh Museum, Rijksmuseum)
- Line 17 — Central Station to Jordaan and Westergasfabriek

### Night Buses (Nachtbus)
- Run Friday and Saturday nights when regular service stops
- Depart from Centraal Station and Amsterdam Sloterdijk
- Supplement ticket required (~€2–3 on top of regular fare)

### GVB 24/48/72/96/hour Tickets
- Unlimited travel within Amsterdam for a fixed price
- Ideal for tourists: 24h = ~€9, 72h = ~€22
- Available at GVB service points, AH to Go, and online

## Rotterdam (RET)

### Metro
- **E line (blue)** — North-South via Beurs (main interchange)
- **D line (red)** — Spijkenisse to Rotterdam Centraal to Capelsebrug
- **C line** — Nesselande to De Akkers

### Trams
- Several lines through the city centre

### Water Bus (Waterbus)
- Connects Rotterdam to Dordrecht and Gorinchem via the Maas river

## The Hague / Den Haag (HTM)

### Tram
- Extensive tram network covering the whole city
- Line 1 — Delft to The Hague Central Station (connects TU Delft students)
- Line 9, 11 — Connect major government buildings and Scheveningen beach

## Payment on Local Transport
- **OV-chipkaart**: Check in when boarding, check out when alighting
- **OVpay**: Tap your bank card or phone directly
- **Day tickets**: Available from drivers/machines for single-day unlimited use
- **Cash**: Most operators no longer accept cash on board

## Cycling as an Alternative
The Netherlands has an exceptional cycling infrastructure. In most Dutch cities, cycling is faster than public transport for distances under 5 km:
- Bike lanes (fietspaden) everywhere
- OV-fiets: Rental bikes available at train stations (requires OV-chipkaart subscription, ~€4/day)
- Swapfiets: Monthly subscription bike service (~€20/month)

## Practical Tips
1. Download the 9292 app — real-time journey planner for all Dutch public transport
2. Always validate your ticket before boarding, even on buses with the driver present
3. Give up priority seats (elderly, disabled, pregnant passengers)
4. Bikes are allowed on most trains (not during peak hours, €7.50/day supplement)
5. Night trams/buses run until the first morning service resumes
