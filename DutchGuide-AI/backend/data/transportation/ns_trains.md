# NS Trains — Dutch Railways (Nederlandse Spoorwegen)

## Overview
NS (Nederlandse Spoorwegen) is the national railway operator of the Netherlands. It operates the largest part of the Dutch railway network, connecting major cities and towns across the country. Trains run frequently — often every 15 minutes between major cities — making rail travel the backbone of Dutch public transport.

## Train Types

### Intercity Direct (ICD)
- High-speed train to Breda and Rotterdam via the HSL-Zuid high-speed line
- Requires a supplement on top of your regular fare
- Connects Amsterdam Centraal → Schiphol → Rotterdam → Breda in about 55 minutes

### Intercity (IC)
- Fast trains connecting major cities with fewer stops
- Runs between Amsterdam, Rotterdam, Utrecht, Den Haag, Leiden, Delft, Eindhoven, etc.

### Sprinter
- Local trains stopping at all stations
- Useful for shorter distances or stations not served by IC trains

### Eurostar & Thalys
- International high-speed trains (Amsterdam to London, Paris, Brussels)
- Require advance booking and separate tickets

## Buying Tickets

### OV-chipkaart (Main Payment Method)
- Reloadable travel card used across all public transport
- Load credit (saldo) and check in/out at yellow card readers
- Requires minimum balance of €20 to board a train
- Single-use paper OV-chipkaart available at ticket machines for one trip

### NS App
- Buy digital tickets on your smartphone
- Available for iOS and Android
- Supports Railrunner (discount tickets booked 1+ day in advance)

### Ticket Machines
- Located at all stations
- Accept debit card, credit card, or cash

### NS Website (ns.nl)
- Book in advance for best prices
- Create an account for subscription discounts

## NS Student Travel (OV-studentenreisproduct)
- Full-time students enrolled at a Dutch educational institution are entitled to free travel
- Choose: free weekday travel OR free weekend travel (not both)
- Activate via DUO (the Dutch student finance organization)
- A €16/month subscription fee applies from your DUO loan

## Tips for New Arrivals
1. Always check in AND check out — forgetting to check out means you pay a penalty fare
2. If you have issues, visit the NS customer service desk (NS Klantenservice) at major stations
3. Download the NS app for real-time journey planner and delays
4. A yellow indicator on the card reader = check in/out successful
5. Inter-city trains are usually on time (>90% punctuality rate)

## Key Stations
- **Amsterdam Centraal** — Main hub, international connections
- **Rotterdam Centraal** — Major hub for south Netherlands
- **Utrecht Centraal** — Geographical centre; connecting point for many lines
- **Den Haag Centraal / HS** — Government city, two main stations
- **Schiphol** — Airport station, direct connection to major cities
- **Leiden Centraal** — Near Leiden University
- **Delft** — Near TU Delft
