# OV-chipkaart & OVpay — Dutch Public Transport Cards

## What is the OV-chipkaart?
The OV-chipkaart (Openbaar Vervoer chip card) is the universal payment card for all public transport in the Netherlands — trains, trams, buses, and metros. Think of it as an electronic ticket that you scan when boarding and alighting any form of public transport.

## Types of OV-chipkaart

### Personal OV-chipkaart (Persoonlijk)
- Linked to your name and photo ID
- Required for certain subscriptions (e.g., NS student travel)
- Can be replaced if lost (balance is recoverable)
- Cost: €7.50 for the card itself

### Anonymous OV-chipkaart (Anoniem)
- Not linked to your personal details
- Cannot be recovered if lost
- Cost: €7.50 for the card itself
- Good for short stays or privacy preference

### Single-Use Paper Card (Papieren OV-chipkaart)
- Disposable card for one journey
- Available from ticket machines and some shops
- More expensive per journey than a regular card

## How to Use the OV-chipkaart

### Step 1: Buy the Card
- Buy at NS ticket machines, GVB/HTM/RET service desks, or online at ov-chipkaart.nl
- Card costs €7.50 (not refundable)

### Step 2: Load Credit (Saldo Laden)
- Top up at ticket machines, NS service desks, Albert Heijn supermarkets (OV-chipkaart machines), or online
- Minimum balance required to check in on trains: €20
- Minimum balance for bus/tram/metro: varies by operator (usually €4)

### Step 3: Check In (Inchecken)
- Hold your card against the yellow card reader at the gate/pole
- Wait for the green light and "beep"
- You're now checked in — the journey starts

### Step 4: Check Out (Uitchecken)
- When you arrive at your destination, hold the card against the card reader again
- The correct fare is automatically deducted
- **⚠️ VERY IMPORTANT: Always check out. If you forget, you are charged a penalty fare (usually €4+ extra)**

## OVpay — The New Way
OVpay is the next-generation contactless payment system replacing the OV-chipkaart:
- Pay with your **bank card, credit card, Apple Pay, or Google Pay** directly
- No need to load credit in advance
- Works on most GVB (Amsterdam), RET (Rotterdam), HTM (The Hague) and NS trains
- Same check-in/check-out principle applies
- Charges are collected automatically from your bank account

## Subscriptions (Abonnementen)
Several discount subscriptions can be loaded onto a personal OV-chipkaart:

| Subscription | Discount |
|---|---|
| Dal Voordeel | 40% off peak + free off-peak |
| Dal Vrij | Unlimited off-peak travel (fixed monthly fee) |
| Altijd Voordeel | 20% discount all day |
| Altijd Vrij | Unlimited travel (premium monthly fee) |
| Studenten OV | Free travel weekdays OR weekends (for students) |

## Peak vs Off-Peak Hours
- **Peak (Spits)**: 6:30–9:00 and 16:00–18:30, Monday–Friday
- **Off-Peak (Dal)**: All other times including weekends and public holidays
- Off-peak travel is significantly cheaper

## Checking Your Balance
- Online at ov-chipkaart.nl (register your card)
- At any NS ticket machine or OV-chipkaart machine
- Via the NS app

## Important Tips
1. Keep at least €20 on your card if you plan to use NS trains
2. Always check out — unchecked journeys result in penalty charges
3. If a machine is broken, inform the driver/conductor — you won't be penalised
4. Report a lost/stolen personal card immediately at ov-chipkaart.nl to protect your balance
5. The card is valid for 10 years from issue date
