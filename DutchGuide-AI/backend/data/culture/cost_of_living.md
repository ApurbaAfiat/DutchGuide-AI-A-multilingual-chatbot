# Cost of Living in the Netherlands

## Overview
The Netherlands is moderately expensive compared to Southern and Eastern Europe, but cheaper than Switzerland, Denmark, or Norway. Amsterdam and The Hague are the most expensive cities. Smaller cities like Eindhoven, Groningen, and Tilburg are notably cheaper.

## Groceries
Supermarket chains in the Netherlands:
- **Albert Heijn (AH)**: Most popular, slightly more expensive but good quality. Basic grocery basket: €50–70/week
- **Jumbo**: Comparable to AH, slight price advantage
- **Lidl / Aldi**: Discount supermarkets, ~20–30% cheaper. Good for basics
- **Dirk**: Budget supermarket, popular in Amsterdam
- **Ekoplaza / Marqt**: Organic/premium, significantly more expensive

Weekly grocery budget (cooking at home):
- Budget: €30–50
- Average: €50–70
- Generous: €70–100

## Housing Costs
See the housing guide for full details. Summary:
- Amsterdam: €700–950/month for student room
- Leiden/Delft: €450–650/month
- Rotterdam: €450–650/month
- Smaller cities: €350–500/month
- Deposit: 1–2 months rent (upfront)

## Transport
- NS student travel card: Free train travel (weekdays or weekends, your choice)
- Without student card: Monthly NS subscription ~€100–130
- City transport day pass: €9–10
- Bike (second-hand): €50–150 one-time purchase (best value)

## Phone Plans (2024)
Monthly SIM-only plans:
- Ben: €10–20/month (5GB–unlimited)
- Lebara: €10–15/month (affordable international calls)
- Tele2: €12–20/month
- KPN / Vodafone / T-Mobile: €25–45/month (premium)

## Utilities

## Healthcare Costs
- Health insurance: €130–150/month
- Annual deductible (eigen risico): €385 (for non-GP care)
- GP visit: FREE (with insurance)
- Dentist (annual check-up): ~€40–80

## Taxes
- If you work in the Netherlands, income tax (inkomstenbelasting) applies
- Rate ranges: 36.97% (up to ~€75,000) / 49.5% (above)
- Students with small incomes often receive a tax refund
- Healthcare and rent allowances can significantly reduce effective costs

## Financial Tips for Students
1. Cook at home — eating out daily adds €200–400/month vs cooking
2. Use Lidl/Aldi for basics, AH for fresher produce
3. Get a second-hand bike instead of a new one
4. Apply for zorgtoeslag (healthcare allowance) — many students qualify
5. Open a free student bank account (Bunq, ING, ABN AMRO all offer free student accounts)
6. Use the OV student card for free train travel
7. Get a CJP card (€17.50/year) for discounts on museums, cinema, concerts
