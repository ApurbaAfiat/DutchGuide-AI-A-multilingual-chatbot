# First Steps After Arriving in the Netherlands

## Your First Week Checklist

Arriving in the Netherlands can be overwhelming. Here's a prioritized action plan:

### Day 1–3: Settle In
- [ ] Find your accommodation and check in
- [ ] Buy an OV-chipkaart or test OVpay for public transport
- [ ] Buy a Dutch SIM card (Ben, Lebara, Tele2, or Vodafone available at supermarkets and phone shops)
- [ ] Familiarise yourself with your neighbourhood
- [ ] Locate the nearest supermarket (Albert Heijn, Jumbo, or Lidl)

### Day 3–7: Official Registration
- [ ] Book an appointment at the local gemeente (municipality) for address registration
- [ ] Gather required documents:
  - Valid passport or national ID
  - Rental contract or letter from landlord/housing provider
  - University enrollment confirmation (for students)
  - For non-EU: residence permit or MVV entry visa
- [ ] Attend the municipality appointment → receive your **BSN number**

### Week 2: Key Admin Tasks
- [ ] **Open a bank account** using your BSN (ING, ABN AMRO, Bunq, or Revolut)
- [ ] **Get health insurance** — mandatory! Compare at zorgwijzer.nl; register within 4 months
- [ ] Apply for **DigiD** online at digid.nl (requires BSN and Dutch address)
- [ ] Register with a **GP (huisarts)** — don't wait until you're sick
- [ ] Activate your **university student card and email**
- [ ] If student: activate your **NS student travel card** via DUO

### Week 3–4: Settling In Further
- [ ] Receive and activate your **DigiD** (activation letter arrives by post)
- [ ] Apply for **zorgtoeslag** (healthcare allowance) if your income is low — use DigiD to apply at belastingdienst.nl
- [ ] Buy a second-hand **bicycle** (Facebook Marketplace, Marktplaats.nl, or university marketplace)
- [ ] Explore your city and university campus

## Opening a Dutch Bank Account

### Required Documents
- BSN number (mandatory)
- Valid passport or ID
- Dutch address (proof)

### Best Options for New Arrivals
| Bank | Student Account | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| ING | Free student account | Best ATM network, app in English |
| ABN AMRO | Free student account | Good online banking |
| Bunq | Free/paid plans | Modern app, quick setup |
| Revolut | Free | Great for international transfers, not a full Dutch bank |

Most banks have English-language apps and customer service.

### Note on iDEAL
iDEAL is the dominant Dutch online payment system, used for almost all online purchases. It works through your Dutch bank account. Without a Dutch bank account, many Dutch services are very difficult to access.

## Getting a SIM Card

### Dutch Operators
- **Ben** — Budget operator, data + calls from €10/month
- **Lebara** — Good for international calls to home country, cheap rates
- **Tele2** — Good data plans, affordable
- **Vodafone / KPN / T-Mobile** — Premium networks with better coverage in rural areas

### Where to Buy
- Supermarkets (Albert Heijn, Jumbo): prepaid SIMs available
- Phone shops (BCC, MediaMarkt)
- Mobile operators' own stores

### What You Need
- Passport or ID to register a SIM (legally required)

## Practical Dutch Phrases for First Days

| Situation | Dutch | English |
|---|---|---|
| Grocery checkout | "Heeft u een bonuskaart?" | "Do you have an AH bonus card?" |
| Asking for help | "Kunt u mij helpen?" | "Can you help me?" |
| At the counter | "Ik heb een afspraak" | "I have an appointment" |
| Bus/tram | "Naar het centrum, alstublieft" | "To the centre, please" |
| Thank you | "Dank u wel" | "Thank you" |

## Common Mistakes to Avoid in Your First Month

1. **Not registering at the gemeente promptly** — delays your BSN and everything that depends on it
2. **Skipping health insurance** — fines start accumulating and you're not covered for healthcare costs
3. **Walking in the bike lane** — dangerous and will frustrate cyclists
4. **Not checking out of public transport** — penalty charges every time
5. **Housing scams** — never pay deposit before signing a contract and seeing the property
6. **Forgetting to eat breakfast** — Dutch lunch is light (sandwiches), so breakfast matters more than back home!
7. **Not locking your bike with TWO locks** — bike theft is extremely common in Dutch cities
