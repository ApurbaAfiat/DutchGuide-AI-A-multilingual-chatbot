# BSN, DigiD & Municipal Registration in the Netherlands

## BSN — Burgerservicenummer (Citizen Service Number)

### What is a BSN?
The BSN is a unique 8 or 9-digit personal identification number used for all interactions with Dutch government services and many businesses. It is equivalent to a social security number in the US or NI number in the UK.

### Why Do You Need a BSN?
You need a BSN to:
- Open a Dutch bank account
- Register with a general practitioner (huisarts)
- Apply for health insurance (zorgverzekering)
- File taxes (belastingaangifte)
- Start working (your employer needs it for payroll)
- Access government services
- Apply for DigiD

### How to Get a BSN

**Method 1: Municipal Registration (for stays over 4 months)**
If you plan to stay longer than 4 months:
1. Find permanent housing first
2. Book an appointment (afspraak) at your local gemeente (municipality)
3. Bring: valid passport/ID, proof of address (rental contract), enrollment confirmation from your university
4. You receive your BSN at the appointment or by mail within a few days

**Method 2: RNI Registration (for stays under 4 months)**
If you stay less than 4 months but still need a BSN:
1. Visit one of 19 designated RNI (Registratie Niet-Ingezetenen) municipalities
2. Amsterdam, Rotterdam, The Hague, Eindhoven, etc. have RNI desks
3. Appointment required — book via the municipality's website
4. Bring: valid passport/ID, documentation of your reason for registration (employment contract, enrollment letter, etc.)

### Important Notes
- You cannot get a BSN without a physical address — sort housing first
- The BSN number is printed on your residence permit (verblijfsvergunning), DigiD letters, and tax correspondence
- Employers, banks, and health insurers will ask for it

## DigiD — Digital Identity

### What is DigiD?
DigiD (Digitale Identiteit) is the Netherlands' digital authentication system. It's like a digital ID you use to log in to Dutch government websites and many official services.

### Why Do You Need DigiD?
You need DigiD to:
- File tax returns online via Belastingdienst
- Access your healthcare information via MijnZorgnet
- Apply for government benefits
- Check your DUO (student finance) account
- Interact with many Dutch government portals

### How to Get DigiD
1. **Get your BSN first** (DigiD requires a BSN)
2. Go to digid.nl/aanvragen
3. Enter your BSN and personal details
4. DigiD sends an activation letter to your registered Dutch address (takes 3–5 working days)
5. Activate your DigiD using the code in the letter

### DigiD App
- Download the DigiD app on your smartphone for easy login
- Enable the app after activating your account on digid.nl

## Municipality Registration (Inschrijving Gemeente)

### What is it?
When you stay in the Netherlands for 4+ months, you must register your address in the BRP (Basisregistratie Personen — Personal Records Database) at your local municipality (gemeente).

### Step-by-Step Process
1. **Find a permanent address** (staying in temporary or hotel accommodation doesn't qualify)
2. **Book an appointment** at your gemeente via their website (look for "inschrijven" or "eerste inschrijving")
3. **Attend the appointment** and bring:
   - Valid passport or national ID card
   - Your rental contract or landlord's written permission
   - For non-EU: valid residence permit or MVV visa
   - Proof of purpose (university enrollment letter, employment contract)

4. **Receive confirmation** — Your address is registered and your BSN is issued (if not already)

### After Registration
- You'll receive a "uittreksel BRP" (municipal extract) confirming your registration
- Update your address with your bank, university, health insurer, and DUO

## IND — Immigration and Naturalisation Service

### What does IND do?
The IND (Immigratie- en Naturalisatiedienst) handles residence permits for non-EU/EEA citizens who want to live, study, or work in the Netherlands.

### EU/EEA Citizens
No residence permit needed. Register at the gemeente after arrival.

### Non-EU Students (Entry Visa + Residence Permit)
1. Your university (as a recognised sponsor) applies for your residence permit on your behalf
2. You receive an authorisation for temporary stay (MVV) if required
3. After arrival, you collect your residence permit (verblijfsvergunning) from IND

### Key Documents for Non-EU Students
- Valid passport
- Proof of enrollment / acceptance letter from a recognised institution
- Proof of sufficient funds (€900+/month)
- TB test certificate (if required for your nationality)
- Health insurance proof

### Contact IND
- Website: ind.nl
- Phone: 088-042 4242 (Monday–Friday 9:00–17:00)
