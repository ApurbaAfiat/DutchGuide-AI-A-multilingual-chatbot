# Amsterdam — City Guide

## Overview
Amsterdam is the capital and largest city of the Netherlands, with a population of ~900,000. Famous for its canals, historic buildings, world-class museums, and liberal culture, Amsterdam is consistently ranked among the world's most liveable cities and attracted 20+ million tourists per year before the pandemic.

## Top Attractions

### Museums
- **Rijksmuseum** — Dutch Golden Age masterpieces including Rembrandt's Night Watch and Vermeer's Milk Maid. Book tickets online (€22.50 adult). Museumplein.
- **Van Gogh Museum** — Largest collection of Van Gogh works globally. Book months in advance (€22 adult). Next to Rijksmuseum.
- **Anne Frank House** — The house where Anne Frank hid during WWII. Deeply moving experience. Book online in advance (€16 adult). Prinsengracht 263–267.
- **Stedelijk Museum** — Modern and contemporary art. Museumplein.
- **Amsterdam Museum / Amsterdam Center for City History** — History of the city.
- **NEMO Science Museum** — Interactive science museum, great for families. Free rooftop with city views.
- **EYE Film Museum** — On the north bank of the IJ, accessible by free ferry from Centraal Station.

### Historic Neighbourhoods
- **Jordaan** — Charming canal neighbourhood with galleries, cafés, and the Saturday Noordermarkt flea market
- **De Pijp** — Young and multicultural; home to the Albert Cuypmarkt (largest market in the Netherlands)
- **Canal Ring (Grachtengordel)** — UNESCO World Heritage Site; iconic canal houses on Herengracht, Keizersgracht, Prinsengracht
- **Vondelpark** — Amsterdam's central park, 47 hectares; free open-air concerts in summer

### Other Highlights
- **Bloemenmarkt** — Floating flower market on Singel canal
- **Heineken Experience** — Interactive brewery tour
- **Artis Zoo** — One of Europe's oldest zoos (1838)
- **Keukenhof** — Day trip from Amsterdam (30 min), most famous tulip garden in the world (open March–May only)

## Getting Around Amsterdam

### Trams
- GVB trams are the main way to get around. Main lines: 2, 11, 12, 17
- Pay with OV-chipkaart or OVpay

### Cycling
- Amsterdam is a cycling city. Bike hire from €10/day (MacBike, Donkey Republic, Swapfiets)
- Follow cycling rules — ride on the bike lane, not the pavement

### Canal Boats
- Public ferry (free): Crosses the IJ between Centraal Station and Amsterdam Noord
- Hop-on hop-off boat tours: ~€20/day

## Practical Tips
- Book popular museums weeks/months in advance — same-day tickets sell out fast
- Respect cycling rules — cyclists have right of way over pedestrians
- Carry a credit card — cash acceptance is declining
- Tap water is safe to drink everywhere in the Netherlands
- English is widely spoken (90%+ of Dutch people speak English)
- Emergency: 112 | Non-emergency police: 0900-8844

## Accommodation Areas
- **Centre (Centrum)**: Most expensive, closest to sights — €100–300+/night
- **De Pijp / Oud-Zuid**: More local feel, 15–20 min to centre — €80–150/night
- **Amsterdam Noord**: Up-and-coming, accessible by free ferry — €60–120/night
