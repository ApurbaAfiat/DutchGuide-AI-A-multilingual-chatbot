# Guide to Dutch Cities

## Rotterdam
The Netherlands' second-largest city and Europe's largest port. Known for its bold modern architecture (unlike any other Dutch city — most historic buildings were destroyed in WWII bombing).

**Must-see:**
- Cube Houses (Kubuswoningen) by Piet Blom
- Markthal — indoor market hall with giant artwork ceiling
- Erasmus Bridge — iconic 800m suspension bridge
- Rotterdam Central Station — dramatic modern architecture
- Boijmans Van Beuningen depot (world's first publicly accessible art depot)

**Getting there:** NS train from Amsterdam (~35 min via Intercity Direct)

## Leiden
A charming university city 35 minutes south of Amsterdam. Home to Leiden University (oldest in the Netherlands, founded 1575). Famous for its canals, windmills, and connection to Rembrandt (born here in 1606).

**Must-see:**
- Burcht van Leiden (castle)
- Rijksmuseum van Oudheden (archaeology museum)
- Hortus Botanicus Leiden (botanical garden, one of world's oldest)
- Windmill De Valk
- Saturday market on Nieuwe Rijn

**For students:** Very student-friendly, affordable compared to Amsterdam, excellent cycling infrastructure.

## The Hague (Den Haag)
The Netherlands' seat of government and home to the International Court of Justice. Has a more formal feel than Amsterdam but excellent museums and nearby beaches.

**Must-see:**
- Mauritshuis — Vermeer's Girl with a Pearl Earring, Rembrandt's The Anatomy Lesson
- Binnenhof — Dutch parliament buildings
- Scheveningen — beach resort district (30 min by tram from centre)
- Escher in the Palace

## Utrecht
The geographical centre of the Netherlands. Lively student city with a beautiful canal system (unique for having shops and restaurants at canal level, below street level).

**Must-see:**
- Dom Tower — tallest church tower in the Netherlands (climb for panoramic views!)
- Dom Church
- Werfkelders (canal cellars with restaurants/bars)
- Museum Speelklok (musical clock museum)

## Delft
Charming historic city between Rotterdam and The Hague. Home to TU Delft and famous for Delft Blue pottery.

**Must-see:**
- Royal Delft pottery factory (authentic Delftware production)
- Vermeer Centrum Delft (Johannes Vermeer was born here)
- Old Church (Oude Kerk) — leans slightly
- New Church (Nieuwe Kerk) — burial place of the Dutch royal family
- Market Square (Markt)

**Getting there:** NS train from Amsterdam (~45 min)

## Giethoorn
A car-free village often called the "Dutch Venice." Connected entirely by canals and footpaths. Exceptionally photogenic.

**Getting there:** Train to Steenwijk, then bus. Or join a day tour from Amsterdam (~90 min).
Best visited: Spring (tulips, green canals) or winter (snow, fewer tourists).

## Keukenhof
The world's most famous tulip garden. Open only from mid-March to mid-May. Located near Lisse, 35 km from Amsterdam.

**Highlights:** 7 million bulbs, 800 varieties of tulips, 32 hectares of gardens.
**Getting there:** Bus from Amsterdam Schiphol Airport or Leiden Centraal.
**Tickets:** Book online in advance (~€22 adult). Sells out!

## Haarlem
A beautiful canal city just 20 minutes by train from Amsterdam. Often overlooked by tourists but beloved by locals. Good day trip from Amsterdam or Amsterdam Schiphol area.

**Must-see:**
- Grote Markt
- Frans Hals Museum (Dutch Golden Age portraits)
- Corrie ten Boom House (WWII history)
- Nearby North Sea beaches (Zandvoort, Bloemendaal) — 15 min by bike or bus
