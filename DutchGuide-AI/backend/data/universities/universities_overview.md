# Universities in the Netherlands

## Overview
The Netherlands has 14 research universities (universiteiten) and 36 universities of applied sciences (hogescholen/HBO). Dutch universities consistently rank among the top in Europe. The medium of instruction is predominantly English at the master's level and increasingly at bachelor's level for international programmes.

## Major Research Universities

### Leiden University (Universiteit Leiden)
- **Founded**: 1575 — the oldest university in the Netherlands
- **Location**: Leiden (30 min from Amsterdam, 20 min from The Hague)
- **Rankings**: Top 100 globally (QS, Times Higher Education)
- **Notable faculties**: Law (top-ranked in Europe), Medicine, Humanities, Social Sciences, Natural Sciences
- **Student population**: ~30,000 students (25%+ international)
- **Campus**: Spread across Leiden city centre + campus Bio Science Park
- **Tuition (EU/EEA 2024)**: ~€2,314/year | Non-EU: ~€10,000–22,000/year
- **Website**: universiteitleiden.nl

### Delft University of Technology (TU Delft)
- **Founded**: 1842
- **Location**: Delft (between Rotterdam and The Hague)
- **Rankings**: Top 50 engineering globally; #1 in Netherlands for engineering
- **Notable faculties**: Aerospace Engineering, Civil Engineering, Architecture, Computer Science, Mechanical Engineering
- **Student population**: ~26,000 students (~30% international)
- **Campus**: Large unified campus with modern facilities
- **Tuition (EU/EEA 2024)**: ~€2,314/year | Non-EU: ~€14,000–20,000/year
- **Website**: tudelft.nl
- **Note**: TU Delft offers some of the world's most-watched online courses on edX

### Erasmus University Rotterdam (EUR)
- **Founded**: 1913
- **Location**: Rotterdam
- **Rankings**: Top 200 globally; strong in Economics, Business, Law, Medicine
- **Notable faculties**: Rotterdam School of Management (top European business school), Erasmus MC (one of Europe's largest medical centres)
- **Student population**: ~32,000 students
- **Tuition (EU/EEA 2024)**: ~€2,314/year | Non-EU: ~€10,000–20,000/year
- **Website**: eur.nl

### University of Amsterdam (Universiteit van Amsterdam — UvA)
- **Founded**: 1632
- **Location**: Amsterdam
- **Rankings**: Top 100 globally
- **Notable faculties**: Economics, Business, Sciences, Humanities, Law, Social Sciences
- **Student population**: ~40,000 students (~30% international)
- **Campus**: Multiple campuses across Amsterdam, including Science Park
- **Tuition (EU/EEA 2024)**: ~€2,314/year | Non-EU: ~€10,000–19,000/year
- **Website**: uva.nl

### Utrecht University (Universiteit Utrecht)
- **Founded**: 1636
- **Location**: Utrecht (geographic centre of the Netherlands)
- **Rankings**: Top 100 globally; strong in sustainability research
- **Notable faculties**: Medicine, Life Sciences, Social Sciences, Humanities
- **Student population**: ~36,000 students
- **Website**: uu.nl

### VU Amsterdam (Vrije Universiteit Amsterdam)
- **Location**: Amsterdam (south, near Zuidas business district)
- **Rankings**: Top 200 globally
- **Notable faculties**: Health Sciences, Economics, Law, Social Sciences
- **Website**: vu.nl

### Eindhoven University of Technology (TU/e)
- **Location**: Eindhoven
- **Notable for**: High-tech industry links, ASML connections, Design department
- **Website**: tue.nl

### Wageningen University & Research (WUR)
- **Location**: Wageningen
- **Notable for**: #1 world for agriculture, food science, environmental sciences
- **Website**: wur.nl

## Tuition Fees 2024–2025

### EU/EEA Students
- Statutory fee (wettelijk collegegeld): **€2,314/year**
- This applies to first bachelor's degree at most universities

### Non-EU/EEA Students (Institutional Fee)
- Typically **€8,000–22,000/year** depending on programme and university
- Medicine and engineering tend to be highest

## Student Finance (DUO)

### Who Can Apply?
- Dutch students
- EU/EEA students working in the Netherlands part-time

### What's Available?
- **Basic grant (basisbeurs)**: No longer available for most students (was abolished in 2023)
- **Supplementary grant (aanvullende beurs)**: Income-dependent, for students from lower-income families
- **Student loan (lening)**: Low-interest loan, repayment starts 2 years after graduation

### OV Student Travel Card
All students enrolled at a Dutch institution qualify for the NS student travel product:
- Free weekday OR free weekend travel
- Must be activated via your DUO account

## Admission Requirements

### Bachelor's programmes
- Dutch language certificate OR English (IELTS 6.0+ / TOEFL iBT 80+) for English-taught programmes
- Academic transcripts
- Application via Studielink (studielink.nl) — the centralised Dutch application platform

### Master's programmes
- Relevant bachelor's degree
- IELTS 6.5–7.0 / TOEFL iBT 90–100 typically required
- Some programmes require GRE/GMAT
- Apply directly to the university

## Student Life
- Universities have active student associations (studentenverenigingen)
- Strong culture of student clubs, sports, and social activities
- Affordable student canteens (mensa) at universities: €3–8 per meal
- Student discounts (with your student card or CJP card): museums, cinemas, restaurants
